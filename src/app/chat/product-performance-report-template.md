# 2. INTRODUCTION

## 2.1 Purpose and Objectives

This Product Performance Report aims to:

1. **Evaluate** current product performance against specifications and market benchmarks
2. **Identify** strengths, weaknesses, and improvement opportunities using DMAIC methodology
3. **Quantify** performance gaps with statistical evidence
4. **Recommend** data-driven improvements aligned with customer needs (Olsen, 2015)
5. **Prioritize** actions based on impact, feasibility, and ROI

## 2.2 Scope of Analysis

### In Scope
- Functional performance testing results
- Production quality data (FPY, DPMO, scrap rates)
- Field performance and warranty claims
- Customer satisfaction metrics
- Cost analysis (manufacturing, warranty, support)

### Out of Scope
- Detailed design schematics (available in design documentation)
- Supplier financial performance
- Market demand forecasting
- Competitive pricing strategy

## 2.3 Methodology Framework

This report integrates three established frameworks:

### A. DMAIC (Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control)
Per Maio (2016), DMAIC provides structured problem-solving:
- **Define**: Problem statement and project charter
- **Measure**: Data collection and baseline metrics
- **Analyze**: Root cause identification
- **Improve**: Solution implementation
- **Control**: Sustained improvement monitoring

### B. DFSS (Design for Six Sigma)
Following Yang & El-Haik (2003), DFSS ensures:
- CTQ (Critical-to-Quality) identification from customer needs
- Robust design optimization
- Design validation through simulation and testing

### C. Lean Product Playbook
Per Olsen (2015), the Lean Product Process ensures:
- Product-market fit validation
- Feature prioritization based on customer value
- Rapid iteration based on feedback

## 6.1 Problem Statement

**Primary Problem**: First Pass Yield (FPY) has declined from 94.6% to 91.6% over the past 12 months, resulting in increased quality costs of $180,000 annually.

## 6.4 5 Whys Analysis

**Problem**: Dimensional defects in height dimension (Cpk = 0.92)

| Level | Question | Answer |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| Why 1 | Why is Cpk below 1.0? | Height dimension trending toward LSL |
| Why 2 | Why is height trending toward LSL? | Cutting tool is wearing faster than expected |
| Why 3 | Why is the tool wearing faster? | New material batch has higher hardness |
| Why 4 | Why is material hardness different? | Supplier changed raw material source |
| Why 5 | Why did supplier change source? | Cost reduction initiative without notification |
