## Rule 2: Questions for Personalization

To create the most effective Finite Element Analysis Report for you, please answer the following questions:

### 1. What is the structure or component being analyzed?
- Is it a static structure (building frame, bridge component, pressure vessel)?
- Is it a dynamic component (automotive part, aerospace component, machinery)?
- Is it a thermal system (heat exchanger, engine component)?
- Or is it a coupled field problem (thermo-mechanical, fluid-structure interaction)?

### 2. What are the loading conditions?
- What types of loads are applied? (Mechanical, thermal, pressure, seismic, vibration)
- What are the magnitude and direction of these loads?
- Are the loads static or dynamic?
- Are there any cyclic or fatigue-loading considerations?

### 3. What material properties are involved?
- What material are you using? (Steel, aluminum, composite, polymer, concrete)
- Are there nonlinear material properties to consider? (Plasticity, hyperelasticity)
- Do you have specific material data sheets or test results?

### 4. What are the boundary conditions and constraints?
- How is the component supported or constrained?
- Are there fixed supports, pins, rollers, or elastic foundations?
- Are there contact definitions between components?

### 5. What is the purpose of this analysis?
- Is this for design validation and verification?
- Is it for optimization and weight reduction?
- Is it for failure analysis or root cause investigation?
- Is it for certification or regulatory compliance?

### 6. What FEA software and version are you using?
- ANSYS (Mechanical, Workbench, APDL)
- Abaqus
- SolidWorks Simulation
- Autodesk Inventor Simulation
- Other (please specify)

### 7. What deliverables do you need?
- Full technical report with methodology
- Executive summary for stakeholders
- Visual post-processing plots and animations
- Design recommendations and optimization suggestions
