## Rule 2: Questions

### 1. Project Overview
- What is the **project name** and **location**?
- What type of project is this (residential, commercial, infrastructure, industrial)?
- Who is the **client/stakeholder**, and what is the approximate **project value**?

### 2. Project Scope & Phases
- What are the **key phases/sequences** of the project?
- What are the major **milestones** and **timeline**?
- What is the **current phase** of construction?

### 3. Identified Hazards & Risks
- What **specific hazards or risks** have already been identified?
- Are there any **areas of particular concern**?
- Have there been any **previous incidents or near-misses** on this project or similar projects?

### 4. Stakeholders & Regulatory Requirements
- Who are the **key stakeholders** involved in this project?
- What **regulatory requirements** and **safety standards** must be followed (e.g., OSHA, local building codes, environmental regulations)?
- Are there specific **permit requirements** related to risk and safety?

### 5. Risk Management Resources
- What **risk mitigation measures** are already in place?
- What **resources** are available for risk management (budget, personnel, equipment)?
- Do you have a preferred **risk assessment methodology** or framework you'd like to use?

## Key References

The following reference materials will be integrated into your Risk Assessment Report:

1. **Petr V. Martens (2015)** - *Risk Assessment and Management in Construction Projects*
   - Comprehensive framework for risk identification, analysis, and evaluation
   - Stakeholder involvement in risk assessment process
   - Tools and techniques for quantifying and prioritizing risks
   - Continuous monitoring and updating of risk assessments

2. **Stephen Grey (2017)** - *Practical Risk Assessment for Project Management*
   - Step-by-step methodology for risk assessment
   - Qualitative and quantitative risk analysis
   - Practical examples and case studies
   - Continuous monitoring and reassessment

3. **Nolberto Munier (2013)** - *Risk Management in Engineering Projects*
   - Risk management frameworks and methodologies
   - Integration into project planning and decision-making
   - Multidisciplinary approach to risk management
   - Effective communication of risks to stakeholders
