## 📋 Questions to Tailor Your Report

**1. Project Overview**

- What is the project name and location?
- What type of structure is being planned (e.g., high-rise building, residential development, bridge, highway, industrial facility, retaining structure)?
- Who is the client/stakeholder, and what is the estimated project value?

**2. Site Conditions**

- What is the current land use and existing conditions at the site (vegetation, existing structures, access constraints)?
- Are there any known topographical features such as hills, valleys, water bodies, or areas prone to flooding?
- What is the approximate site area and dimensions?

**3. Proposed Construction**

- What is the proposed structure type, number of stories, and approximate gross floor area?
- What are the anticipated structural loads (dead loads, live loads, seismic loads)?
- What is the proposed foundation depth (basement, shallow foundations, deep foundations)?

**4. Geotechnical Concerns**

- Are there any known geotechnical issues at or near the site (soft soils, high groundwater, slopes, historical landslides, contamination)?
- Has any previous geotechnical investigation been conducted at the site? If so, can you provide the findings?
- Are there any specific geological hazards of concern (seismic activity, liquefaction potential, subsidence)?

**5. Project Requirements**

- What is the project timeline and any critical milestones?
- Are there specific design codes or standards that must be followed (e.g., ASCE 7, ACI 318, local building codes)?
- Do you have any specific concerns or priorities for the geotechnical analysis (cost optimization, accelerated construction, specific foundation type preference)?
