## Understanding Your Traffic Impact Study Needs

A Traffic Impact Study (TIS) is a comprehensive analysis that evaluates the potential traffic effects of a proposed development, land use change, or transportation project. It identifies potential congestion, safety concerns, and infrastructure needs, providing recommendations to mitigate negative impacts and improve the overall transportation system.

The study typically includes:

- **Existing Conditions Analysis**: Current traffic volumes, road network characteristics, and intersection operations
- **Trip Generation**: Estimated traffic volumes to be generated by the proposed development
- **Trip Distribution & Assignment**: How new traffic will be distributed across the road network
- **Capacity Analysis**: Assessment of roadway and intersection capacity under future conditions
- **Impact Assessment**: Identification of impacts and mitigation measures
- **Conclusions & Recommendations**: Strategies to maintain acceptable levels of service

## Key Questions to Personalize Your Study

To create a tailored Traffic Impact Study that meets your specific needs, please provide information on the following:

**1. What is the nature of your proposed project or development?**

Please describe the type of development (residential, commercial, industrial, mixed-use, institutional, etc.), its size (number of units, square footage, etc.), and location. Is this a new development, expansion of an existing facility, or a change in use?

**2. What is the study area boundary?**

Identify the geographic scope of your study. Which intersections, road segments, and access points will be analyzed? Will the study focus on immediate site access or include wider network impacts?

**3. What are the existing transportation conditions?**

Please describe the current road network, including road classifications, number of lanes, traffic control devices, transit service, pedestrian and bicycle facilities, and any existing traffic data you may have (ADT volumes, peak hour volumes, crash history).

**4. What are the applicable standards and requirements?**

Are there specific local, regional, or national standards that must be followed? Any ITE (Institute of Transportation Engineers) guidelines, state DOT requirements, or municipal ordinances? Are there specific level-of-service thresholds or mitigation requirements?

**5. What is the project timeline and approval process?**

When is the study needed? What are the regulatory review requirements? Are there any critical deadlines or phased development plans?
