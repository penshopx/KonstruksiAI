## Rule 2: Questions

To create a comprehensive and tailored Design Risk Assessments Document that meets your specific needs, please provide details on the following:

### 1. What is the design/project being assessed?
Please describe the product, system, or structure that requires risk assessment (e.g., mechanical assembly, structural framework, industrial equipment, consumer product, etc.).

### 2. Who are the primary stakeholders?
Identify who will use this document (e.g., project managers, design engineers, safety officers, regulatory bodies, clients, etc.) and their level of technical expertise.

### 3. What is the scope of the risk assessment?
Clarify whether this is:
- A new design
- A design modification

- Other (please specify)

### 4. What specific risks should be addressed?
Please identify any particular concerns or risk categories you want covered:
- Structural integrity risks
- Safety hazards
- Environmental impact

- Maintenance accessibility
- Material failure modes
- Human factors
- Regulatory compliance

### 5. What format and standards should be followed?
Specify any particular:
- Industry standards (ISO, ASME, ANSI, etc.)

- Desired output format (formal report, checklist, matrix, etc.)
